1. Context

The initiative is positioned within a globally chartered financial institution operating across the United States, European Union, United Kingdom, and Asia-Pacific. The target function is Oracle database performance tuning inside IT/Database Site Reliability Engineering, moving from reactive DBA-led optimization to proactive, AI-enabled tuning.

Sector & Business Type

Retail and wholesale banking, investment services, and enterprise payments in a multi-jurisdiction regulatory environment.

Department, Process & Function

Oracle database performance tuning within IT/Database SRE, with AI recommending optimizations and Oracle validating them before DBA approval.

Problem Statement

The document frames the core issue as an unsustainable manual tuning model, with engineers spending more than 27 hours per week on troubleshooting, while legacy optimization approaches deliver limited efficiency at enterprise data scale.

2. Technology Choice

The solution uses a frontier LLM for reasoning and recommendation generation, while preserving deterministic control by keeping Oracle as the validation engine. The design emphasizes zero-data-retention controls, explainability, secure orchestration, prompt caching, semantic routing, and strict separation between AI reasoning and execution.

Primary AI Selection Criteria

  • Zero Data Retention (ZDR)
  • Reasoning quality and explainability
  • Safety architecture
  • Enterprise controls and governance features
  • Ease of integration with on-prem MCP

Architecture Principles

  • Probabilistic reasoning is separated from deterministic execution
  • Semantic routing assigns simpler vs. more complex workloads
  • Every AI suggestion is mathematically validated before use
  • Prompt caching and structured payloads reduce cost and variance

4. Security Plan

Security is organized around the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 and strengthened by the NIST AI RMF and ISO/IEC 42001 and 27001. The design assumes generative AI risk is real and controls it through sanitization, least privilege, monitoring, rollback, and an emergency kill switch.

Govern & Identify

  • ZDR contracts before any provider API use
  • AI acceptable use policy and annual attestation
  • Full data flow mapping for GDPR and DORA
  • OWASP Top 10 for LLMs threat model coverage

Protect

  • Mandatory SQL sanitization with bind variable replacement
  • Least-privilege Oracle automation account
  • Vault-managed secrets with 90-day rotation
  • TLS 1.3 and certificate pinning

Detect, Respond & Recover

  • Malicious syntax scanning for DDL/DML risk
  • API billing anomaly monitoring
  • Emergency kill switch to sever LLM connectivity
  • Automated rollback to known-good baselines

6. Scaling Strategy — Crawl, Walk, Run

The implementation follows a phased maturity model that increases autonomy only as controls, trust, and evidence mature. Each phase defines its scope, oversight model, controls, and measurable gates before progressing.

Crawl (Months 0–3)

  • Non-production only
  • Advisory mode with PDF recommendations
  • 100% DBA review
  • Gate: ≥70% precision and zero sanitization failures

Walk (Months 3–6)

  • Selected production databases
  • Low-risk pre-approved patterns
  • Dual approval for higher tiers
  • Gate: ≥95% regression avoidance and no P1 incidents

Run (Months 6–12+)

  • Enterprise-wide scoped deployment
  • Limited auto-implementation for approved patterns
  • Rollback automation and quarterly DR drills
  • Gate: ≥40% DBA productivity gain and exam readiness

Governance Principle

AI recommends — Oracle validates — DBAs approve — systems audit.

7. Governance and Compliance

Governance is structured across five pillars: cross-functional oversight, AI use-case inventory, model risk management, regulatory compliance, and third-party vendor governance. The design is built for multi-jurisdiction banking environments.

Cross-Functional AI Oversight Committee

  • CISO: API security, TLS, vendor review, kill switch authority
  • DPO / Legal: GDPR, CCPA, GLBA, ZDR, DPIA, cross-border transfers
  • Head of DBA / DBRE: validation logic, restricted accounts, rollback, DR testing
  • Model Risk Management: validation, inventory, drift monitoring, change control
  • Internal Audit: controls assurance and examination readiness

Classification

AutoTune AI is classified as Minimal/Limited Risk under the EU AI Act and as Medium Operational Risk / Elevated Data-Handling Risk / Low Customer-Harm Risk under SR 11-7.

Conclusion

The capstone concludes that AI-driven SQL tuning is strategically sound, measurably beneficial, and regulatory-defensible when implemented with strong controls, human oversight, deterministic validation, and examination-ready governance.

“AI recommends, Oracle validates, humans approve, and systems audit.”

Every action is intended to be traceable, reversible, and aligned to concrete obligations spanning GLBA, SR 11-7, GDPR, DORA, and the EU AI Act.
